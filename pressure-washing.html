  • Extends the usable life of your concrete

How We Work

Our Pressure Washing Process

Site Walk and Scope

We walk the property to identify problem areas, heavy staining, and any surfaces that need special attention. You get a clear scope and price upfront.

Pre-Treatment

For oil stains and heavy buildup, we apply a degreaser or cleaning agent before washing. This breaks down tough contaminants so the pressure wash can remove them completely.

High-Pressure Wash

We use commercial-grade pressure washing equipment with surface cleaners for even coverage. No streaks, no missed spots, just clean concrete.

Rinse, Recovery, and Inspection

We rinse the area and take steps to prevent wash water from entering storm drains, including containment and recovery where required. We check for any remaining spots and confirm the work meets the agreed scope before we wrap up.

Common Questions

Pressure Washing FAQ

Do you pressure wash buildings or just concrete?

We specialize in concrete surfaces only. That includes parking lots, sidewalks, loading docks, dumpster pads, drive-throughs, garage floors, and any other concrete flatwork. We do not wash building exteriors, siding, or roofs.

Can you remove oil stains from a parking lot?

Yes. We pre-treat oil stains and heavy grease with commercial-grade degreasers before pressure washing. Most oil stains come out completely or are significantly reduced. For very old or deep stains, we will let you know upfront what to expect.

How often should commercial concrete be pressure washed?

Most commercial properties benefit from at least one thorough pressure wash per year, typically in the spring after winter salt and grime have built up. High-traffic areas like restaurant drive-throughs or loading docks may need quarterly cleaning. We can set up a recurring schedule that fits your property.

Where do you offer pressure washing services?

We serve the greater Omaha metro area including Bellevue, Papillion, La Vista, Gretna, Elkhorn, Ralston, Council Bluffs, and surrounding communities. We work on commercial properties throughout Nebraska and western Iowa.

Should I seal my concrete after pressure washing?

It is highly recommended. A freshly pressure-washed surface is the ideal time to apply a sealer because the pores are clean and open. Sealing after washing locks in the clean appearance and protects the concrete from future staining and weather damage. We offer both services and can do them in a single visit.
